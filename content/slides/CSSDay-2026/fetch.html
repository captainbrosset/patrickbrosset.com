var request = new ActiveXObject("Microsoft.XMLHTTP");

Quick example with Fetch: Back in 2000, the Outlook team at Microsoft needed some way to do background requests from JS. They made an ActiveX experiment called XMLHTTP, which came with IE5. Later, it got used in weird ways, together with other APIs, And that's what made GMail possible, and the whole Web 2.0 era. The XMLHTTP ActiveX was later reverse-engineered and implemented in other browsers. And this eventually standardized later as XMLHttpRequest. And it doesn't end here, because then, much later, we got a new, better version, as the Fetch API. And now we just have that primitive of the platform, that we just couldn't do without. It goes to show that this obscure thing which the outlook team needed and then got used in a new way by other devs, leading to new use cases, which then led to a standard (XHR), and then to a better standard (Fetch).
