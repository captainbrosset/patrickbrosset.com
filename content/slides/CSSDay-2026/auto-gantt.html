One more, this time to show the a row orientation grid-lanes layout. Each team here is a separate lane, and items are assigned to specific lanes. Certain tasks, like sprint planning span all lanes. What's cool about this one is how these spanning tasks end up pushing or pulling other tasks. If I make a task from team A longer, it ends up pushing the sprint planning, resulting in a planning change for all teams. Conversely, if I remove tasks from team B, the sprint planning can happen sooner. Same if I delete the sprint planning task, tasks for all teams can be scheduled sooner. Grid-lanes does this balancing act on its own, simply by its very nature of trying to fill lanes.
