In CSS, here's what masonry is, at a high level: 1. There's a new name: grid-lanes, get used to it! 2. It's not a special kind of grid. It does share some things with CSS grid, but it's fundamentally different in how things are placed. 3. Grid-lanes is more like 1D, like flexbox. You only control the lanes of your layout, not the other direction. 4. Grid-lanes isn't only vertical It can be horizontal too. And it's not only top to bottom. It can be reversed. 5. You don't have to have same-size lanes only. Lanes can be of any size and different from each other. In fact, the full power of the `repeat()` function and fr units can be used. 6. This algorithm is opportunistic, and sometimes can seem chaotic. That's the default placement. But you can still control the placement of items if you want. You can assign items to specific lanes. Or make them span multiple lanes. 7. Visual order is not DOM order. Items that appear later in the DOM may appear visually above earlier items. Same with flexbox and grid. So beware of keyboard tabbing order.
